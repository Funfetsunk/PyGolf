## 🕹️ Game Concept
**Title:** Golf! 
**Genre:** Sports game (golf)  
**Platform:** Desktop  
**Perspective:** 2d, top down.

**Core Idea:**  
A simple looking golf game with click and drag shot mechanics. The player creates a golfer and plays through their career. They start as an amatuer, through semi-pro, then through the pr tours to reach the PGA (although we will have to change names like the PGA for copyright reasons). Also a simple golf game it will include some slightly advanced mechanics like shot shaping.
The player will be able to increase their stats, play harder courses and buy new clubs as they progress.

---

## 🎮 Gameplay Mechanics
- Player controls: Mouse controls, buttons to click to change clubs/ shot-type/ shot shaping. Click, drag back and click to start shot, set power and make shot
- Objective: As with any golf game, the player is trying to get around hte course in the fewest shots possible. The bigger picture is the player trying to get into the "PGA Tour" and win the 4 majors and become world no. 1.
- Core mechanics:
  - Golf courses have all the usual trapping: tee boxes, fairways, bunkers, water, rough, trees. These should all have relevant impact on distance and accuracy
  - Mouse controls will be used to control playing golf
  - Between rounds of golf the player will have a chance to spend currency they earn playing either on training (stat increaes) or clubs. Eventually when they become professional they can hire staff to increase their skills and get sponsership to bring in money. Those sponsers will expect things in return.
  - Each tour (amatuer, semi-pro, pro etc... ) Will have it's own shedules and courses. The player will work through that shedule and, if they are good enough at the end win a card onto the next tour. When they turn pro they start earning points towards PGA world rankings

- Win condition: On a small scale they win by getting the lowest score against simulated golfing opponents on the tour. On a grander scale they win the 4 majors and become world no.1
- Lose condition: The opposite of the above.

## 🧩 Features
Include the following features:
- Shot shaping, players can play draws and fades
- Playing out of different terrains (bunkers, rough, fairways) changes the shot distance and accuracy
- The player is working there way from amatuer to professional golf. Aiming, in the end to win all 4 majors and be world number 1. The game plays out the entire career of a golfer.
- As an amatuer the player will play in local tounemants trying to get access to the semi-pro tour
- As a semi-pro the player will be on a tour that has a set schedule of events at certain golf courses. They are trying to be the best on the tour to qualify for the next level up.
- There is 1 amatuer level, 2 semi-pro levels and 3 pro levels (the last pro level being "The PGA")
- Between rounds of golf players can use their winning to increase there stats (take lessons) and buy better equipment. Eventually (on the pro tour) the player will be able to hire staff (coaches, caddies, sport psychologists etc..) to improve parts of their game as well.
- Although some courses will appear on multiple tours their will be a lot of different courses in the game
- Each tour will have a set group of competitors. Although you don't see them play, they all play the same events as you. You are trying to beat their scores. Each golfer on each tour will have a name and some stats. This means that the same golfers in the tour will do better than others and you will be competing in an overall leagure table to win. As you move through the tours the golfers get better.

Optional 
- Sound effects, golf noises should be added to the game, hitting the ball, swinging the club, nature sounds, the ball bouncing. At the higher levels, gentle crowd noises.
- Scoring should be tracked for the player just like regular stroke play golf. Each tour will have it's own overall league system too
- There are no levels like a conventional game. Lots of golf courses and tours to work through.
- It is vital that the game saves after every round of golf so the player doesn't lose their progress.
